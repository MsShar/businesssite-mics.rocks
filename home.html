JULLY BLACK - FEATURED ARTIST

BIO

Singer, Songwriter, Television Host, Actor: Jully Ann Inderia Gordon, known as Jully Black, Born in Toronto Canada November 8, 1979. At the age of 7 she sang for a church choir which opened up doors to the music industry. Collaborations: Choclair, Kardinal Offishall and Baby Blue Soundcrew, Keith Harris, drummer of the Black Eyed Peas. Label:Warner/Chappell Music: Wrote songs for Destiny's Child and Nas. Albums: This Is Me, Revival (dedicated to her sister who died at the age of 24. Her sister was the one who helped her to become a singer). Opening Acts for: Jay-Z and 50 Cent. Acting: production Da Kink In My Hair at the Princess of Wales Theatre in Toronto. Television: Host; eTalk on CTV. Television version of Da Kink In My Hair, she also sang the theme song. Awards: 3 times Juno Awards Nominee in the category, R&B Single of the Year.
